The Bioreactor
The ALGAE System is a revolutionary new approach to food production that operates at the highest trophic (biological efficiency) level possible. The high productive capabilities of ALGAE and the optimized grow system Hydrovia created represents a turning point in agriculture that pivots away from factory farms and thousand-mile food shipments and towards a new methodology of radically locally-sourced (can you get more local than your roof) food. Algae Production
The ALGAE system is a primary producer, meaning it converts sunlight, CO2, and a few nutrients directly into food ideally suited for human consumption. This allows ALGAE to be much more efficient than traditional agriculture systems, enabling more localized and decentralized production of food, reducing transportation costs and pollution, and giving power back to the people. At a base level, the ALGAE system has only a few inputs- oxygen and CO2, water, sunlight, and a custom-made nutrient blend to keep the algae healthy and growing rapidly. Everything input into the ALGAE system is eventually consumed by the human user-- the algae converts sunlight into food and nutrients into vitamins and minerals to keep you strong!

    Malnutrition is often neglected as an American problem, yet malnutrition negatively impacts an already strained healthcare system. Supplementing the American diet with algae can eliminate malnutrition, making stronger, healthier, and longer-living Americans. In fact, algae as a food might have some of the most significant helath benefits of any food, reducing diabetes, hypertension, and eliminating protein deficits. Do the healthy thing, and eat algae!
  • $1 Trillion
    Saved From Erosion
    Agriculture has many costs hidden downstream, and few are more significant than erosion. Current agriculture practices are eroding the fertile land globally at an alarming rate. This unsustainable farming cannot be sustained, hurts already challenging fish populations, and contaminates drinkng water through runoff. By saving land from agriculture use, we can reduce erosion, improving the health of our waterways and ensuring we have healthy soil for generations to come.
